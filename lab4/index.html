Making Sourdough Bread from Scratch

  1. Make Sourdough Starter
    • Ingredients Needed
      • 5lb bag all purpose flour
      • 1/2 cup whole wheat flour
      • Warm water
    • Instructions (minimum 7 days)
      1. Add 1/2 cup wole wheat flour and 1/4 cup warm water.
      2. Mix until well combined.
      3. Cover and leave in 75-80 F for 48 hours.
      4. Remove 1/2 of your starter.
      5. Add 1/2 cup of all purpose flour.
      6. Add 1/4 cup of warm water.
      7. Mix unil well combined.
      8. Cover and leave in 75-80 F for 24 hours.
      9. Repeat steps 4-8 for the next 3 days.
      10. If starter has doubled in size and smells pleasent, it is ready to use.
      11. Patience is key!
  2. Make Sourdough Bread
    • Ingredients Needed
      • 5.35 oz of active sourdough starter
      • 8.80 oz warm water
      • 0.90 oz olive oil
      • 17.65 oz bread flour (not all purpose flour)
      • 0.40 oz fine sea salt
      • Fine ground conrmeal, for dusting
    • Instructions
      1. Make the dough
        1. Whisk together the starter, water, and olive oil in a large bowl.
        2. Add he flour and salt.
        3. Squish together with your hands until all the flour has been absorbed.
        4. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or very damp kitchen towel.
        5. Let rest from 30 minutes up to 1 hour.
        6. Work the dough into a rough ball for about 15 seconds.
      2. Bulk Rise
        1. Cover the bowl with wrap or a very damp kitchen towel.
        2. Let rest in a warm spot to rise for 3-12 hours or until it has doubled in size.
        3. Optional: Start 30 minutes into the bulk rise. Gather a portion of the dough, stretch it upwards and then fold it over itself. Rotate the bowl ¼ turn and repeat this process until you have come full circle to complete 1 set. Do this once or twice spaced about an hour apart. This will increase the total volume and height of your bread.
      3. Cut and Shape the Dough
        1. Divide your work surface in half; lightly flour one side (for cutting) and leave the other half clean (for shaping).
        2. Remove the dough from the bowl, and place onto the floured section so that it does not stick. You do not need to ‘punch down’ the dough; it will gently deflate as you fold and shape it.
        3. Cut the dough in half to make 2 loaves, or leave it whole for a single loaf.
        4. To shape, use a bench scraper to move your dough to the non-floured section (if there is any flour present, it will be difficult to shape- brush away any excess). Starting at the top, fold the dough over toward the center. Give it a slight turn, and then fold over the next section of dough. Repeat until you have come full circle.
        5. Flip the dough over and place it seam side down. Using your hands, gently cup the sides of the dough and rotate it, using quarter turns in a circular motion. You can also pull it towards you to even out the shape. Repeat this process until you are happy with its appearance.
      4. Second Rise
        1. Place dough inside oven for 30 minutes to 1 hour and cover with the lid of a pot or very damp cloth. The dough is ready when it is slightly puffy but not double in size.
        2. Preheat your oven to 450º F towards the tail end of the second rise.
      5. Score the Dough
        • Cuts should be about 2-3 inches long and 1/4 inch deep.
      7. Bake the Dough
        1. Place he bread into the oven on the center rack (lid on).
        2. Reduce temperature to 400 F.
        3. Bake for 20 minutes.
        4. Remove the lid and continue to bake for 40 minutes or until depp, golden brown.
        5. Check internal temperature of bread, should be 205-210 F.
        6. Remove the bread from the oven, and cool on a wire rack for at least an hour before slicing.
